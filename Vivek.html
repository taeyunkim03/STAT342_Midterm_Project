MLE: The Championship Strategy

01. The Core Intuition

MLE isn't just math, it's how we get the best guess. We look at our observations and work backward to find the most likely cause.

The Mystery Die: You roll a "1". You have three dice in your pocket:

  • 4-Sided Die: 25% chance of rolling a 1.
  • 6-Sided Die: 16.7% chance.
  • 20-Sided Die: 5% chance.

Since the 4-Sided Die makes your roll most probable, it is our Maximum Likelihood Estimate.

02. The Mathematical Playbook

The Likelihood Function L of theta treats the data as fixed and the parameter theta as the variable we can tweak to find the peak.

$$L(\theta) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i | \theta)$$

The Strategy: We use the Natural Log (ln) to turn that product into a sum. Itâ€™s easier to differentiate and keeps the peak at the same exact location.

03. Finding the Peak

To find the best theta, we look for the highest point on the curve. Mathematically, that's where the slope (derivative) equals zero:

$$\frac{d}{d\theta} \ln L(\theta) = 0$$

04. Game Day Application

Imagine the Seahawks kicker has 10 field goal attempts. He Makes 7 and Misses 3. What is his "true" success probability theta?

$$L(\theta) = \theta^7(1-\theta)^3$$

By maximizing this function, we find that the most likely value for his success rate is exactly the observed ratio:

$$\hat{\theta}_{mle} = \frac{7}{10} = 70\%$$

The 70% rate is the parameter that makes that specific season performance the most probable outcome.
